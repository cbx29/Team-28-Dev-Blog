Initial Project Objectives

A list of the key criteria that our end-product should meet:

  • Detect and track gestures for atleast 2 players
  • Develop a range of demonstratable game examples
  • Added functions to the existing MFC settings panel.
  • Generate an energy report from data collected during the game.

Q1: Who are our end users?

Schools with kids in games (e.g. webcam on wall, tv or projector on wall showing a game, 2 or more children playing), wards with sick children in a hospital who need exercise for rehab, special needs clinics with children who need to play with others, elderly in care homes playing with their friends, friends at home playing games for exercises in fitness competitions.

Q2: What is the end product that we need to create?
  • A new settings panel or added functions to the existing PseudoVR, facial navigation and multitouch MFC settings panel.
  • A game engine with Wii-sports like games.
  • Demonstratable game examples.
  • API for adding more motion games. E.g. archery, skiing
Q3: What goals do you expect from the end product?

Population health, for everyone. Including those with accessibility needs. Social engagement with others through playing games. Smooth multiplayer experiences. Simple, easy to use navigation from start to finish, crash resilience and knowing what level of hardware is cheapest to run effectively (most efficient). Reporting back to intel with the performance reports for their testing teams.



Q1: What is your main motivation for playing our game?

You get to move around and have fun while enjoying the game so it’s healthier compared to sitting down.

Q2: What is your favorite game and what do you enjoy about it?

I love Minecraft because there is so much you can do, and it is simple to understand.

Q3: What features do you look for most?

It would be very fun if I could play with my 3 other friends and compete.



Q1: How can we ensure that our game is accessible to you and other patients?

Being able to recognize a wide range of different motions, as different patients will have different ranges of motion.

Q2: What kind of exercises do medical professionals advise for you?

I do lots of hand movement exercises to regain full control and mobility of the hand that I broke, but I am not allowed to do contact sports.

Q3: What features do you look for most?

A user profile section that displays previous scores and your progression so that I can track my recovery progress.



Q1: How proficient is your use of technology and which systems are you familiar with?

I am not very proficient, but I am familiar with WhatsApp and Facebook. I have also played arcade games like Pacman before.

Q2: What is your fitness level like and what kind of sports can you comfortably play?

I am able to stand for short durations so I can play games that are quick and low intensity.

Q3: What kind of games do you enjoy?

I enjoy games that are more strategic and tactical so I would prefer this to a physically intense game.
