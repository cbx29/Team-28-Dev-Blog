Requirements

Improving Accessibility in Gaming

Get Started

Project Background

UCL MotionInput is a touchless computing system that enables users to interact with their computer using physical gestures captured by their web camera. The system was designed to enhance accessibility and provide a more intuitive way of interacting with digital content. However, the previous version of MotionInput was limited in its multiplayer capabilities, which hindered its use in gaming scenarios. Our team identified this limitation and set out to develop a solution that would enable multiplayer interactions in gaming.

Our solution involved redesigning the hand and body modules of MotionInput to detect multiple bodies and sets of hands, and to map these detected body parts into specific events that can be added to newly created multiplayer gaming modes. This allowed us to create new gaming modes that were specifically designed for two players, thus enabling users to engage in multiplayer gaming scenarios without the need for physical controllers or input devices.

The new architecture we developed not only allowed for improved accessibility in gaming but also had potential applications in other fields such as physiotherapy. The gestures used to play our games provide an excellent way of exercising injured limbs and joints, on the condition that the user is supervised by a physiotherapist. Overall, our team successfully enhanced the functionality of MotionInput to better serve the needs of users, particularly in the gaming industry.

Project Goals

A list of the key criteria that our end-product should meet:

  • Detect and track gestures for atleast 2 players
  • Develop a range of demonstratable game examples
  • Add functions to the existing MFC settings panel.

Requirement Gathering

We extensively spoke with our project coordinator, Professor Dean Mohamedally, to determine the scope of our project. Through these discussions, we received helpful guidance on our development requirements. As a result, we were able to create Multiplayer Modes by building on preexisting modes within MotionInput, and also coordinated with other teams to build Multiplayer versions of their work.

Throughout the project, we maintained constant communication with Professor Dean, and adjusted our project requirements accordingly. We discussed both our successful and unattainable (due to technical reasons) development goals. This ensured that both our team and Professor Dean were always on the same page. When a development goal was unattainable, we consulted with Professor Dean to explore alternative solutions. Consequently, our project requirements were amended from time to time.

Q1: Who are our end users?

Schools with kids in games (e.g. webcam on wall, tv or projector on wall showing a game, 2 or more children playing), wards with sick children in a hospital who need exercise for rehab, special needs clinics with children who need to play with others, elderly in care homes playing with their friends, friends at home playing games for exercises in fitness competitions.

Q2: What is the end product that we need to create?
  • A new settings panel or added functions to the existing PseudoVR, facial navigation and multitouch MFC settings panel.
  • A game engine with Wii-sports like games.
  • Demonstratable game examples.
  • API for adding more motion games. E.g. archery, skiing
Q3: What goals do you expect from the end product?

Population health, for everyone. Including those with accessibility needs. Social engagement with others through playing games. Smooth multiplayer experiences. Simple, easy to use navigation from start to finish, crash resilience and knowing what level of hardware is cheapest to run effectively (most efficient). Reporting back to intel with the performance reports for their testing teams.

MoSCoW Requirement List

  • Must Have

    • Modify hand and body landmark detectors such that they identify pairs of left and right hands
    • Ensure that the modified hand and body landmark detectors are backward compatible with single player detection
    • Ensure that code is extendable – can easily implement new multiplayer game modes
    • Develop Multiplayer Adventure Extremity Mode
    • Develop Multiplayer Drive Mode
    • Develop Multiplayer Punch Mode
    • Develop Multiplayer Foot Mode
    • Develop Multiplayer Gun Mode
    • Implement MFC
    • Compilation into DLL
    • Intuitive and accessible gestures to use
    • Good Time performance – fast and smooth for users
  • Should Have

    • Improve accessibility further by adding gamepad height adjustment
    • Improve aesthetics of interface my updating extremity buttons

  • Could Have

    • Generate an energy report that can be sent to Intel
    • Detection and tracking of 3 or more users

  • Won't Have

    • Implement multiplayer games that map to mouse controls
