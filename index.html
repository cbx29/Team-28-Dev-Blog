Abstract

Multiplayer Interactions demonstrated with an Open Source PC Sports Game Engine

MotionInput V3, the 3rd generation of UCL MotionInput, is a touchless computing system that allows users to control and interact with the computer using physical gestures captured by their web camera. However, this version was only able to map the physical gestures of a single user. This inhibited individuals from using MotionInput to engage in Multiplayer gaming, and that was the exact problem our group identified and looked to solve.

Our solution was to create as many 2-player gaming modes as we could based on the MotionInput gaming modes that were available for us to exploit. What we did was to redesign the core module to detect multiple bodies / sets of hands, and to map these detected body parts into specific events that can be added to newly created multiplayer gaming modes.

Upon completion of our development timeline, we implemented 6 different multiplayer gaming modes for 2 players. We tested these modes across a range of games (driving, adventures, boxing, shooting, etc.) and they worked well within our 2-player gaming environment. Our solution allows users to use MotionInput in a more communal setting, thus increasing the utility they would derive in gaming through MotionInput. The specific event movements we track can also be integrated into a range of patients’ physiotherapy routines. Finally, the architecture we created, will allow future teams to effortlessly extend current modes and integrate new modes.
