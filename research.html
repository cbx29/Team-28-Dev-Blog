Project Research

Multiplayer Interactions demonstrated with an Open Source PC Sports Game Engine

The current implementation of MotionInput v3 is limited in that it does not provide support for multiplayer functionalities. Specifically, in the detection stage of each module, there is no provision to accommodate situations where the media pipe detects multiple body parts. Similarly, in the events and events handler portion of each module, no additional consideration is given to multiplayer functionalities.

However, it is noteworthy that the media pipe technology does offer support for the detection of multiple hands, with each hand segmentation resulting in a set of 21 3D landmarks. Consequently, it is possible to incorporate the multiple hands' feature into the motion input project.







That being said, MediaPipe does not currently support multiple-body detection, which presents a challenge for implementation. To address this challenge, the current solution involves isolating the images of each person and applying media pipe processing to each individual image. While this approach yields high accuracy, it also slows down the speed of the motion input due to the additional processing required. Nonetheless, this method provides a possible direction for consideration, such as splitting the screen to accommodate multiple users.

Technology Research

To address the challenge of detecting multiple bodies, we have considered several potential solutions.

The first solution considered involved requiring players to wear specific target items, allowing us to cut out the background around the target. However, this solution runs counter to the foundational principle of motion input, which aims to provide a camera-based input solution that does not require additional equipment. Consequently, this approach was deemed unsuitable.

A second solution involved incorporating the yolov5 library to detect the person and then isolate their image for processing. However, we found that integrating another library resulted in significant slowdowns in motion input performance, rendering this approach unfeasible.

Ultimately, we arrived at a solution based on user behavior. When playing multiplayer games, users typically stand side-by-side without needing to move in the game. As such, we proposed splitting the screen into two parts and applying segmentation separately, with player one being the person on the left side of the screen. This approach leverages user behavior patterns and does not require any additional equipment or libraries, making it a viable solution to the challenge of detecting multiple bodies.

Solution Summary

Multiplayer Interactions demonstrated with an Open Source PC Sports Game Engine

In summary, our team has successfully expanded the MotionInput project to incorporate multiplayer functionality, achieving our goals of providing users with a truly immersive hands-free device interaction experience with their friends. This breakthrough not only allows individuals with disabilities to participate in group exercises and engage in multiplayer gaming but also broadens the range of games that can be played with MotionInput.

Our team is excited to have accomplished this major milestone and to have opened up new opportunities for users to engage with each other in a whole new way. With this latest advancement, the possibilities for MotionInput are endless, and we look forward to continuing to innovate and push the boundaries of what is possible in the realm of hands-free device interaction.
